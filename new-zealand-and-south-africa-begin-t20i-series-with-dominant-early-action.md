# [New Zealand and South Africa Begin T20I Series With Dominant Early Action](https://8jjsports.com/new-zealand-and-south-africa-begin-t20i-series/)

**New Zealand and South Africa** have officially kicked off their **five‑match Twenty20 International (T20I) series** in New Zealand, marking an important bilateral tour early in the 2026 international calendar. The series started on **March 15 at Bay Oval in Mount Maunganui**, following fresh squad announcements and strategic rotations that give opportunities to emerging talent from both sides. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

In the **series opener**, **South Africa’s men’s team delivered a strong performance**, chasing down New Zealand’s modest total of **91 all out** and sealing a **seven‑wicket win** with 20 balls to spare. Connor Esterhuizen top‑scored with **45 runs**, while South Africa’s bowling attack dismantled the home line‑up early. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}

The women’s fixture on the same day saw **New Zealand Women post a convincing 80‑run victory** over South Africa Women, powered by an aggressive innings from Amelia Kerr. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

The full T20I schedule runs through **March 25**, with matches planned at iconic venues including **Seddon Park (Hamilton), Eden Park (Auckland), Sky Stadium (Wellington)** and **Hagley Oval (Christchurch)** — giving fans almost two weeks of cricket across the series. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}
