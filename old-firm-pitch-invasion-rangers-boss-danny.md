# Old Firm Pitch Invasion: Rangers Boss Danny Röhl Condemns Fan Behaviour

Rangers head coach Danny Röhl criticised the crowd trouble that erupted after Celtic defeated Rangers in a dramatic Scottish Cup quarter-final clash at Ibrox.

Celtic progressed to the semi-finals after winning **4-2 on penalties** following a **0-0 draw after 120 minutes** in one of the most intense Old Firm encounters of the season. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}  

However, the match was overshadowed by chaotic scenes at full-time when supporters from both clubs entered the pitch, forcing police and stewards to intervene.

## Police confirm arrests after disorder

Police Scotland confirmed that several arrests were made following what officials described as a “despicable display” of behaviour. Missiles were reportedly thrown while officers formed barriers between rival supporters to restore order inside the stadium. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}  

Authorities also said an investigation would examine incidents before the match, including supporters entering the Broomloan Road stand without tickets, which caused security delays.

## Röhl condemns pitch invasion

Speaking after the match, Röhl said he had not witnessed the incident directly but strongly condemned the behaviour once he became aware of it.

> “I was not on the pitch in the moment. I did not see it until now. Nobody likes to see this,” Röhl said. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}  

The Rangers manager added that the derby atmosphere had been excellent during the match itself and insisted such incidents should have no place in football stadiums.

## Celtic boss fears incident will overshadow victory

Celtic manager Martin O’Neill also acknowledged the chaos following the final whistle. He suggested the emotional nature of the derby and the excitement of a dramatic penalty shootout contributed to supporters spilling onto the pitch.

Despite the disturbance, Celtic secured their place in the **Scottish Cup semi-finals**, while Rangers were left to reflect on a frustrating exit after dominating much of the game but failing to score.
