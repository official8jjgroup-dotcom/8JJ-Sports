# Africa T20 Cup Plan: 5 Big Updates on New Tournament

Cricket administrators are moving forward with ambitious plans to establish a new **Africa T20 Cup**, a continental Twenty20 competition designed to strengthen the sport across African nations.

Here are the **five big updates** on how the tournament is shaping up:

## 1. Continental T20 Cup Concept Takes Shape

The **Africa Cricket Association (ACA)** is exploring a structure for a continental T20 Cup similar to the **Asia Cup**, aiming to generate revenue and support cricket development throughout the region. This proposal reflects a growing drive to boost the profile of cricket in Africa. 

## 2. Initial Tri‑Series Set for 2026

Before the full‑scale competition launches, a **tri‑nation T20 series** featuring **South Africa**, **Zimbabwe**, and **Namibia** is planned for **August 2026**. This mini‑tournament will serve as a precursor to the Africa Cup and help build momentum and interest. 
## 3. Six‑Team Tournament on the Horizon

The longer‑term vision is to expand into a **six‑team Africa Cup**, with South Africa, Zimbabwe, and Namibia joined by three additional sides that are expected to qualify via regional qualification tournaments. This structure aims to make the competition more competitive and comprehensive. 

## 4. Support from Top African Cricket Nations

With established teams like South Africa and Zimbabwe involved early, the tournament could attract strong support — both on the field and commercially. Their participation is seen as crucial to the event’s success and long‑term viability. 
## 5. Timeline and Launch Expectations

Organisers currently expect that **the full Africa T20 Cup won’t begin until 2027**, following the tri‑series and once scheduling and qualification details are finalised. Discussions are ongoing about the best window for the event and how to align it with the international calendar. 

---

The Africa T20 Cup plan represents a major step forward for cricket in the continent, promoting competitive opportunities and greater visibility for emerging teams across Africa.
