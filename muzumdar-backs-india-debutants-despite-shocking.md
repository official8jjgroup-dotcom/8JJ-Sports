# Muzumdar Backs India Debutants Despite Shocking WACA Test Loss

India Women head coach Amol Muzumdar defended the performances of several debutants despite India suffering a heavy 10-wicket defeat to Australia in the one-off pink-ball Test at the WACA Ground in Perth. :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}  

The match ended inside three days as Australia dominated the contest in challenging conditions under lights. India were bowled out for 198 in the first innings and 149 in the second, allowing Australia to chase down a small target without losing a wicket. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}  

Despite the disappointing result, Muzumdar focused on the positives, particularly the performances of the four players who made their Test debuts during the match — Pratika Rawal, Kashvee Gautam, Kranti Gaud and Sayali Satghare. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}  

Rawal impressed with a determined knock in the second innings, scoring 63 runs from 137 balls to record a maiden Test half-century. Satghare also made a strong impression with the ball, claiming 4 wickets for 50 runs in Australia’s first innings. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}  

Lower-order batter Kashvee Gautam contributed 34 valuable runs in the first innings, while Kranti Gaud bowled with intensity despite the difficult conditions at the pace-friendly Perth venue. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}  

Muzumdar said the team showed strong character even though the result did not go their way.  

> “A bit disappointing with the result, but I’m really happy with the fight that we showed for the last three days,” Muzumdar said after the match. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}  

He also highlighted the challenge of playing with the pink ball under lights at the WACA, where the ball moved significantly and created problems for batters. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}  

India’s coach added that a better first-innings total might have changed the outcome of the match, but he credited Australia for producing a dominant performance in their home conditions. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}  

Despite the defeat, Muzumdar believes the experience gained by young players in demanding overseas conditions will benefit the team as it prepares for future international fixtures. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}
