# Aryaman Birla Appointed RCB Chairman After Aditya Birla Group Leads Historic IPL Franchise Takeover

Royal Challengers Bengaluru have entered a new era of ownership after a **consortium led by the Aditya Birla Group, Times of India Group, Bolt Ventures, and Blackstone** completed a landmark acquisition of the franchise valued at **$1.78 billion**.

As part of the restructuring, **Aryaman Vikram Birla** has been appointed as the new **chairman of RCB**, marking one of the most high-profile leadership appointments in IPL franchise history.

## Historic Ownership Change for RCB

The takeover sees United Spirits Ltd divest full control of the franchise, ending its long-standing ownership of one of the IPL’s most followed teams.

The consortium deal includes both the **men’s and women’s RCB teams**, with regulatory approval from the **BCCI** expected to formalise the transition in the coming days.

This acquisition places RCB among the most valuable sporting franchises globally and signals a major shift in the IPL’s commercial landscape.

## Aryaman Birla Steps Into Leadership Role

Aryaman Birla, son of Aditya Birla Group chairman **Kumar Mangalam Birla**, will now oversee the strategic direction of the franchise.

A former domestic cricketer and current business executive within the Aditya Birla Group ecosystem, Aryaman has previously held leadership roles across multiple group ventures, including fashion, hospitality, and investment platforms.

His appointment is seen as a move to blend corporate leadership with a modern sporting vision for RCB’s next phase.

## Consortium Behind the Deal

The ownership group brings together major global and Indian investors:

- Aditya Birla Group (lead partner)
- Times of India Group
- Bolt Ventures (David Blitzer-led sports investment firm)
- Blackstone (global private equity giant)

The structure is expected to combine financial strength with global sports management expertise, particularly through Blitzer’s experience in multi-franchise ownership across international sports.

## Strategic Vision for RCB

Early indications suggest the new ownership aims to expand RCB into a **global sports brand**, leveraging cross-franchise links and international sporting networks.

The inclusion of investors with stakes in other global teams is expected to influence long-term commercial and sporting strategies, including talent development, analytics, and brand expansion.

## What Happens Next

With regulatory approval pending, the focus now shifts to structural integration ahead of the upcoming IPL season.

RCB’s new leadership group is expected to outline its long-term roadmap once the takeover is fully ratified, with attention on both sporting performance and franchise growth.

For fans, the change marks the beginning of a new identity era for one of the IPL’s most iconic teams.
