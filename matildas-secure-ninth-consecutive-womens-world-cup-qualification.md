# [Matildas Secure Ninth Consecutive Women’s World Cup Qualification](https://8jjsports.com/matildas-secure-ninth-consecutive-womens-world-cup/)

Australia’s **Matildas** have officially booked their place in the **2027 FIFA Women’s World Cup** after a dramatic **2–1 victory over North Korea (DPR Korea)** in the **AFC Women’s Asian Cup 2026 quarter‑finals** in Perth. The win wasn’t just about reaching the last eight of the continental tournament — it also secured their **ninth consecutive World Cup qualification**, a feat the team has maintained since its first appearance in 1995.

The match was a tightly contested affair, with **Alanna Kennedy** breaking the deadlock early before **Sam Kerr** doubled the lead shortly after halftime. Despite a second‑half reply from North Korea, the Matildas held on through disciplined defending and crucial saves from goalkeeper **Mackenzie Arnold** to register the historic result.

The qualification ensures that Australia will again be among the global field at the **FIFA Women’s World Cup™ in 2027 in Brazil**, where the Matildas will look to build on their deep runs from recent international tournaments and push for a first ever World Cup title.

Fans and pundits alike lauded the team’s resilience in the Asian Cup, noting that securing World Cup qualification — especially in front of a passionate home crowd — sets a strong tone for the squad’s preparations in the months ahead.
