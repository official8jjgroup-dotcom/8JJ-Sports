# Carabao Cup Final: Arsenal Selection Error at Wembley Sparks Major Tactical Debate

Arsenal’s hopes of lifting the **2026 Carabao Cup** were ended at Wembley after a **2-0 defeat to Manchester City**, with the aftermath dominated by intense scrutiny over Mikel Arteta’s selection decisions. The match has quickly become a talking point not only for the result, but for the tactical choices that shaped the outcome.

Manchester City capitalised on key moments in the second half, with **Nico O’Reilly scoring twice in quick succession** to secure the trophy and extend City’s dominance in domestic cup competitions.

## Goalkeeper Decision Under the Spotlight

The biggest controversy centred around Arteta’s decision to start **Kepa Arrizabalaga** ahead of first-choice goalkeeper **David Raya**. Kepa, who had featured in earlier rounds of the competition, was handed the final but struggled to cope with the intensity of the occasion.

A crucial error early in the second half, where Kepa failed to deal with a routine ball into the box, directly contributed to Manchester City’s opening goal and shifted the momentum decisively in their favour.

## Manchester City’s Clinical Response

City’s ability to punish mistakes once again proved decisive. After a tight first half, they raised the intensity immediately after the break, with **O’Reilly’s quick double strike** effectively ending Arsenal’s challenge.

The performance underlined City’s reputation for delivering in high-pressure finals, particularly under **Pep Guardiola**, where game control and efficiency in decisive moments remain a defining feature.

## Arsenal’s Missed Opportunities

Despite spells of possession and early attacking intent, Arsenal failed to convert key chances in the first half. Players in advanced positions were unable to break down City’s defensive structure, leaving the Gunners frustrated as the match progressed.

As the game slipped away, Arsenal struggled to regain rhythm, with City’s control in midfield limiting transition opportunities.

## Tactical Debate and Managerial Pressure

Following the defeat, discussion has focused heavily on Arteta’s selection approach and whether loyalty to squad rotation in cup competitions may have come at a cost in a major final.

While the Arsenal manager defended his decisions, the outcome has reignited debates around whether clubs at elite level should prioritise consistency in selection for high-stakes matches.

## What the Result Means Going Forward

The defeat ends Arsenal’s Carabao Cup campaign and adds further pressure to their pursuit of silverware this season. While they remain competitive across other competitions, the Wembley loss highlights the fine margins that define elite-level knockout football.

For Manchester City, the victory reinforces their continued dominance in domestic cup football, adding another trophy to their recent success under Guardiola.
