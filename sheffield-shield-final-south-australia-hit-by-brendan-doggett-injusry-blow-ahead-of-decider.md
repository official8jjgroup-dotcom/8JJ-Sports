# Sheffield Shield Final: South Australia Hit by Brendan Doggett Injury Blow Ahead of Decider

South Australia have been dealt a significant setback ahead of the **Sheffield Shield final against Victoria**, with key fast bowler **Brendan Doggett ruled out due to a hamstring injury**. The setback comes at a crucial stage of the season, forcing the reigning champions to rethink their bowling structure just days before the decider.

Doggett’s absence removes one of South Australia’s most reliable pace options — a bowler who has played a central role not only in wicket-taking but also in controlling the flow of innings across multiple phases.

## A Major Structural Loss for South Australia

Doggett’s injury is more than just a personnel issue for South Australia. The fast bowler has been a key stabilising force in the attack, capable of operating with the new ball, maintaining pressure in the middle overs, and returning in the death overs without disrupting the team’s rhythm.

His absence leaves a noticeable gap in both control and consistency, forcing the coaching staff to redistribute responsibilities across the remaining pace unit.

## Tactical Rebalancing Ahead of the Final

With Doggett unavailable, South Australia are expected to adjust their bowling combination, potentially relying more heavily on rotation-based seam options and additional spin support depending on pitch conditions.

This shift increases tactical pressure on the leadership group, who must now manage overs and match-ups without their most adaptable pace anchor.

## Depth Concerns Under the Spotlight

The injury also exposes concerns around squad depth in the fast bowling department. While South Australia still possess quality seam options, replacing Doggett’s multi-phase control is not a straightforward task.

The challenge now lies in maintaining structural balance across all phases of the innings, particularly against a strong Victoria batting lineup known for sustained pressure and disciplined shot selection.

## Final Approaches With Increased Uncertainty

As the Sheffield Shield final approaches, South Australia remain competitive, but their pathway has become more complicated. Doggett’s absence not only affects wicket-taking potential but also disrupts the structural blueprint that has underpinned their success in recent seasons.

The final now presents a test not just of skill, but of adaptability under sudden tactical disruption.
