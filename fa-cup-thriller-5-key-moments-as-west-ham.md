West Ham produced a dramatic performance in the FA Cup as they edged past Brentford after a tense contest that required penalties to decide the winner. Here are five key moments that defined the thrilling cup tie.

## 1. Early breakthrough for West Ham  
West Ham started strongly and took the lead through Jarrod Bowen, who converted from the penalty spot after VAR intervened to award the hosts a spot-kick. The early goal helped the Hammers establish control in front of their home supporters. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}  

## 2. Brentford fight back  
Brentford responded well and equalised through a powerful header from Nathan Collins following a dangerous cross into the box. The visitors showed resilience and refused to let West Ham dominate the match. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}  

## 3. Another twist as Brentford level again  
The match continued to swing back and forth when Brentford drew level again, with Igor Thiago converting a penalty to make it 2-2 and send the game into extra time. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}  

## 4. Tense extra-time stalemate  
Neither side could find a winning goal during extra time despite several attacking attempts. The deadlock meant the match had to be decided by a dramatic penalty shootout. :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}  

## 5. Decisive moment in the shootout  
The decisive moment came when Brentford’s Dango Ouattara attempted a risky Panenka penalty that was easily saved by West Ham goalkeeper Alphonse Areola. Konstantinos Mavropanos then scored the winning penalty to secure victory for the Hammers. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}  

West Ham’s dramatic victory keeps their FA Cup hopes alive as they progress to the quarter-final stage, where they will face Leeds United in the next round.
