# Rayudu Slams CSK’s Poor Planning Against RCB in IPL 2026

Former India and Chennai Super Kings cricketer **Ambati Rayudu** pulled no punches in his assessment of CSK’s tactical approach after their loss to **Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB)** in **IPL 2026**. Rayudu said that CSK’s planning was not just flawed — the team *stuck to the bad plan* instead of adapting as the match unfolded.

Rayudu noted that while poor execution can sometimes be forgiven, sticking rigidly to a strategy that isn’t working is far more problematic. He pointed out that CSK’s bowlers continued with the same plan even when it was being repeatedly punished by RCB’s batsmen, and that there was no senior intervention or pause to reassess and guide bowlers on how to respond.  

According to Rayudu, senior players on the field should have taken a moment to slow things down, communicate with bowlers, and adjust tactics rather than just follow the original plan blindly. He emphasised that in high‑pressure T20 situations, a team must be flexible and responsive rather than rigid.  

Rayudu also highlighted specific areas where CSK’s execution failed, particularly in death overs bowling where they conceded heavily, and stressed that better on‑field leadership and real‑time decision‑making could make a critical difference in tight games.  

The comments followed CSK’s defeat where RCB posted a huge total and CSK’s bowlers were unable to apply effective adjustments as the opposition accelerated, ultimately contributing to the Super Kings’ defeat in that match.
